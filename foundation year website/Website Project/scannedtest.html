Are scanned exams good for testing knowledge?


With lockdown in place, universities have tried to carry out their assessments online. With written parts of the tests being scanned at home before being submitted online.We will discuss how effective it at replicating real life test submission and whether is a true reflection of a student's potential.Scanned exams involve a student capturing their exam script using a phone or similar device then uploading that for marking.It does have a benefit such as allowing a greater variety of answers to be written than just ticking boxes in an online mcq, which allows for a greater reflection for a student's knowledge.However it's main problem is that many technical difficulties can arrise when scanning the answers or uploading them,though it has been mitigated through extra time it just allows more people to take advantage of the extra time.Overall its a good option if there are no other possible ways of conducting written exams.
